Welcome to our summer project due to Covid-19

Our group’s mission

Solving challenge questions due to COVID-19 let us feel like we are a part of something bigger. While we heard about this challenge from Queens’ mathematics & statistics department, since our original summer plan failed, we grouped together and thought about how to apply current knowledge background to the real-life problem. Our group has a diversity from mathematics, economics, statistics and computer science, we can analyze data from economic and health organizations by using predicting models, data mining, and other data analysis/science tools to predict the trend for the future. We are a team of self-taught, quick learners with excellent academic records, eager to solve challenging questions for the real-world. So that is the reason for why we participated in the program.

What’s our solution?

Our group’s purpose is analyzing the risk factors of spreading disease in Canada such as masks and social distance. We made an interactive interface to show the effects of these risk factors in the second page, and show our analysis results and great tips for Covid-19 in the third page.
